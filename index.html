What is mean by hsl and hsla ?

HSL and HSLA are color models in CSS used to specify colors in terms of hue, saturation, and lightness, with an optional alpha (opacity) channel in HSLA. These formats are often more intuitive for designers since they describe colors similarly to how we perceive them visually.

    Hsl

  • Hue: Represents the type of color on a color wheel and is measured in degrees from 0 to 360. Common values:
  • 0° = Red
  • 120° = Green
  • 240° = Blue
  • Saturation: Defines the intensity or vividness of the color, ranging from 0% (a shade of gray) to 100% (full color).
  • Lightness: Adjusts the brightness of the color, from 0% (black) to 100% (white).

    • Hsla

  • HSLA adds an alpha (opacity) channel to HSL, allowing control over transparency. The alpha value ranges from 0 (completely transparent) to 1 (fully opaque).
